Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

In creating on a digital coding medium such as Visual Studio Code, it can be difficult to tap into the creative aspects of the mind. There is a lack of immediate visual changes and rather, you build block by block. So finding ways to focus my creative energy into how the code was arranged and presented was key. Rather than thinking in terms of changing layers and correcting blemishes, the creativity was focused on choosing how to package the code.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging with others was a crucial step in the creation process. Often when working with code, it can be easy to miss something, so having a second set of eyes serves crucial in uncovering small blemishes. Along with this, gaining ideas and inspiration from other's work was instrumental in creating the final projects.

3. Think deeply about craft.

Throuhgout this phase, I have learned that you can't go into a coding workflow without a plan. Trying to plan things out along the way doesn't work because of how precise the code is. To ensure the best outcome without error, you have to think deeply and plan out a course of action. An example of this is learning how to wireframe for potential projects.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

Coming into this phase, I had a novice understanding of code, however, the latter two projects required more knowledge to complete. To figure out how to produce the visuals I wanted to, I had to engage in research to find tips to follow. This served vital in improving the quality of my work, as well as taught me the importance, and ease, of finding sources online to reference and learn from.

5. Learn to present your work

Learning how to package and present work is crucial to the appeal your design has on the audience. When working with code, this becomes even more important. Presenting work in a code sense is how the code appears on a digital medium. This means that presenting the work is the final stage in execution.
